What is Bakugan?

The Bakugan franchise consists of children's toys, video games, and tv shows. It originally aired in Japan back in April 5, 2007. Fortunately, the tv channel Cartoon Network decided to premiere Bakugan in the United States on February 24, 2008. The first series consists of 52 episodes, and is titled Bakugan Battle Brawlers.
The story begins with random cards falling from the sky. Once children from all over the world picked up these cards, bakugan would materialize in front of them out of thin air. The main villain for the first half of the show is Masquerade, who showed up one day and began sending people's Bakugan permanently into the doom dimension. Dan and his friends try to track down Masquerade before he can send any more Bakugan to the doom dimension.

Gate Cards

  1. Gate cards are set up before the match begins.
  2. The area covered by the gatecards designate the playing area.
  3. A gate card can be activated at any time by the person who set it on the field.
  4. Properly leveraging gate cards can turn the tide during intense brawls.
  5. A gate card's effect is limited only to the Bakugan standing on it.

Bakugan Stand

  1. Players must throw their Bakugan so that they land on the gate cards.
  2. Positioning is important. Bakugan can only battle other bakugan on the same gate card.
  3. You may stand multiple bakugan on the same gate card. Their powers can then be added up together.

Ability Cards

  1. Ability cards are cards you can use at any time without having to set them beforehand.
  2. Ability cards have a wide range of effects and they're the most important aspect to deciding the outcome of a Bakugan brawl.
  3. Multiple ability cards can be used at the same time. Using 2 abilities simultaneously is called performing a double ability, and using 3 is a triple ability.
